Laura Moses is a Ph.D. Candidate in the Department of Political Science at The Ohio State University and is expected to defend her dissertation in June 2022. She is on the job market for the 2021-2022 academic year.

Laura's research examines how digital platforms change and affect politics using network analysis, machine learning, and natural language processing. Her research examines the ways digital platforms shape politics and our political activities. Her dissertation develops a theory and measurement of interest actors who are unique, elite individuals that engage in influential political activities, especially on social media, and examines how interest groups and interest actors impact the public sphere. Broadly, she is interested in computational techniques that expand the possibilities of social science inquiry. She also has research in progress that examines how Members of Congress use speech, the role of search platforms in opinion formation, and interest group representation.

She is the Senior Scholar for the Program In Statistics and Methodology and a Graduate Fellow for Institute for Democratic Accountability and Engagement (IDEA) .

Laura received an MPA from Brown University specializing in data-driven policy and holds BA's in Music and History. Please find her CV here.
